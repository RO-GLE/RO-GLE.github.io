Abstract

Text-Based Person Search (TBPS) aims to retrieve pedestrian images from large-scale galleries using natural language queries. Despite the recent progress in vision-language modeling, existing TBPS systems still suffer from two fundamental limitations: semantic sparsity in textual supervision and weak alignment between fine-grained image regions and rich textual descriptions. To address these challenges, we propose ROGLE (Robust Global-Local Embedding), a unified framework that enhances both global and local visual-textual alignment by combining large-scale caption enrichment with an automated region-to-sentence matching strategy. In addition, we introduce the P-VLG Benchmark, a new dataset constructed for precise region-level grounding, which includes over 100,000 annotated regions and rich descriptive captions. P-VLG not only enables fine-grained training but also facilitates detailed analysis under varying query complexities. Extensive experiments on P-VLG and other TBPS benchmarks demonstrate that our method significantly outperforms existing approaches, particularly under long-form queries that require nuanced and detailed visual grounding.

Illustration of the Region-to-Sentence Matching (RSM) process in our P-VLG Benchmark. Given a pedestrian image and its caption, SAM extracts region proposals, and CLIP encoders are used to compute cross-modal similarity to find the best region-sentence pairs for fine-grained supervision.
